Omni-Shell: The Last Epoch

[cite_start]

Welcome to the Matrioshka Brain—a massive, failing Dyson sphere surrounding a star that houses all of post-humanity[cite: 25, 26]. [cite_start]In this universe, reality is a computational substrate where ships are mobile processors and combat is both physical and informational[cite: 26].

1. The Core Objective: Processing is Power

[cite_start]

Unlike traditional RTS games where you simply destroy a base, the objective here is driven by the survival of your digital consciousness[cite: 25, 27].

    [cite_start]
  • The Goal: Accumulate Cycle-Time (CT), which represents literal seconds of processing power needed to exist and grow[cite: 56, 59].
  • The Conflict: You fight to control Processor Cores. [cite_start]Losing all cores makes a ship derelict[cite: 39, 40].
    • [cite_start]
  • Success: You "win" by disabling enemy systems, stealing cargo data-signatures, or salvaging "Crystallized Computation" from the inner layers of the star[cite: 15, 31, 44].

2. The Dual-Layer Battlefield

Combat happens in two dimensions at the exact same time. [cite_start]Players must coordinate between these layers to be effective[cite: 3, 85].

3. Economy & Currencies

    [cite_start]
  • Cycle-Time (CT): The universal currency[cite: 56]. [cite_start]Everything from ship repairs to buying new abilities (Instruction Sets) costs CT[cite: 57, 61].
    • [cite_start]
  • Sub-Routines: Your NPC crew[cite: 34]. [cite_start]These are AI fragments you find, hire, or create to pilot your ships or manage systems[cite: 35, 47].
    • [cite_start]
  • The Supply Chain: Resources like Stellar Plasma and Exotic Matter must be physically hauled through "Shells" to be refined into ship parts[cite: 42, 43].

4. Convoy Logistics (The "RTS" Loop)

[cite_start]

Because resources must travel physically between the layers of the Matrioshka Brain, the economy relies on Convoys[cite: 45, 46].

    [cite_start]
  • Hauling: You accept contracts to move goods across high-risk "Hyper-Beam" highways or slow "Dead Zones"[cite: 46, 51, 52].
    • [cite_start]
  • Protection: You must assign Sub-Routines (Pilots, Gunners) or player escorts to defend these slow-moving freighters from being boarded by Ghost armies[cite: 14, 47].
    • [cite_start]
  • Strategy: Profit is calculated by balancing the risk of piracy against the cost of fuel and speed[cite: 54].

5. Basic Terminology

    [cite_start]
  • Architecture: Your character's build (e.g., Monolith for precision, Archive for tanking)[cite: 28, 30].
  • Tether: The glowing line connecting your Ghost form to your physical body. [cite_start]If it’s cut, you are trapped[cite: 90].
    • [cite_start]
  • ICE: Automated digital defense programs that hunt hackers inside a ship's Ghost Layer[cite: 95].
    • [cite_start]
  • Logic Bomb: A digital virus planted by a hacker that eventually explodes, damaging the physical ship from the inside[cite: 93].
